Sleep, Stress, and Mental Health: A Predictive Analysis

📌 Project Overview

This project investigates how sleep patterns, lifestyle habits, and physical activity influence stress levels and mental well-being.

Using statistical modeling and exploratory data analysis, the study identifies the strongest predictors of stress while accounting for demographic and health-related factors.

🎯 Research Questions
1. Is there a relationship between sleep patterns and stress levels?
2. How do lifestyle habits influence stress after controlling for demographic and health factors?
3. How does physical activity affect stress levels?
4. Which predictors have the strongest association with mental well-being?


🛠️ Tools & Technologies
- Python
- Pandas
- NumPy
- Seaborn
- Matplotlib

Statsmodels
- Ordinal Logistic Regression
- Jupyter Notebook


📊 Methodology

Data Preparation
- Cleaned and prepared survey data
- Handled missing values
- Encoded categorical variables

Exploratory Data Analysis
- Examined relationships between sleep, lifestyle habits, and stress
- Visualized trends and distributions

Statistical Modeling
- Applied Ordinal Logistic Regression
- Conducted hypothesis testing
- Calculated odds ratios for interpretation


📈 Key Findings

Sleep Patterns
- Longer sleep duration was associated with lower stress levels.
- Higher sleep quality significantly reduced the likelihood of severe stress.

Lifestyle Habits
- Screen time was the most consistent predictor of increased stress.
- Smoking showed a weaker relationship with stress.
- Alcohol intake showed no strong independent relationship with stress.

Physical Activity
- Exercise type influenced stress outcomes.
- Yoga and Pilates were associated with lower stress levels.

Demographics
- Males showed higher stress likelihood than females.
- Older individuals tended to experience higher stress levels.


📌 Conclusion

Sleep quality and screen time emerged as the strongest predictors of stress. The findings suggest that improving sleep habits and reducing excessive screen exposure may contribute to better mental well-being.
